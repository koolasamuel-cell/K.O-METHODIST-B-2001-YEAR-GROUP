About K.O.M.B. 2001

K.O. Methodist 'B' Year 2001 Old Students Association is a non-profit and non-political association formed by past students who completed in the year 2001. United by our shared history, values, and experiences, we are committed to strengthening the bond among members, promoting welfare, supporting one another, and contributing meaningfully to the development of our Alma Mater.

🎯 Our Mission

To unite all K.O. Methodist 'B' Year 2001 past students and provide a common platform for mutual support, welfare, friendship, and cooperation while promoting the growth of our Alma Mater.

🌟 Our Vision

To become a strong, respected, and impactful alumni association that positively influences the lives of its members, contributes to educational development, and serves as a model of unity.

🤝 Core Values

Unity, Integrity, Transparency, Accountability, Mutual Support, Respect, Service, and Commitment to the welfare and progress of every member.

👥 Membership

Open to all past students of K.O. Methodist 'B' JHS who completed in 2001 and are willing to uphold the Constitution and objectives of the Association.

🏫 Our Commitment

We are dedicated to initiating and supporting development projects that benefit K.O. Methodist 'B' JHS and future generations of students.

💬 Common Forum

A platform where members can deliberate on matters affecting their welfare, share ideas, build friendships, and work together for the advancement of the Association.

📜 K.O.M.B. 2001 CONSTITUTION

PREAMBLE

We, the past students of K.O. Methodist 'B' Junior High School who completed in the year 2001, conscious of our shared history, values, and collective potential, and desirous of establishing a strong, united, and well-structured Association, do hereby resolve to form ourselves into an Association governed by this Constitution.

We believe that unity and organized cooperation will enable us to support one another, address common challenges, promote welfare, contribute meaningfully to the development of our Alma Mater, and serve our Motherland. Accordingly, we adopt and promulgate this Constitution as the supreme guiding document of the Association.

ARTICLE I: NAME OF THE ASSOCIATION

The Association shall be known as K.O. Methodist 'B' Year 2001 Old Students Association, hereafter referred to as K.O.M.B. 2001.

ARTICLE II: OBJECTIVES

  1. Operate as a non-profit and non-political association.
  2. Promote unity, welfare, and mutual support among members.
  3. Provide a common forum for deliberating on matters affecting members.
  4. Unite all past students of K.O. Methodist 'B' JHS who completed in 2001.
  5. Initiate, support, and undertake development projects for the Alma Mater.
  6. Establish cordial relations with other year groups and relevant associations.
  7. Undertake any lawful activities deemed beneficial to the Association.

ARTICLE III: MEMBERSHIP

  1. Membership shall be open to all past students of K.O. Methodist 'B' JHS who completed in the year 2001.
  2. Members shall be considered in good standing upon fulfillment of obligations approved by the Association.

ARTICLE IV: REGISTRATION, DUES AND CONTRIBUTIONS

  1. There shall be no mandatory registration fee.
  2. The Association's funds shall be derived from approved contributions, donations, and other lawful sources.
  3. Contributions may be levied as and when necessary, subject to approval.
  4. All excess funds shall be deposited into the Association's approved bank account.

ARTICLE V: GOVERNING BODIES

The Association shall be governed by:

  1. Patrons
  2. Executive Committee

ARTICLE VI: EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE

The Executive Committee shall comprise:

  1. President / Chairperson
  2. Vice President / Vice Chairperson
  3. Secretary
  4. Finance Secretary
  5. Organizer
  6. Patrons (not less than three (3))

ARTICLE VII: FUNCTIONS OF EXECUTIVE OFFICERS

Section 1: President / Chairperson

  1. Serve as Chief Executive Officer of the Association.
  2. Preside over all meetings.
  3. Enforce and uphold this Constitution.
  4. Represent the Association at all official functions.
  5. Call emergency meetings when necessary.
  6. Propose committees and assign duties.

Section 2: Vice President

  1. Assist the President in the performance of duties.
  2. Act in the absence or inability of the President.
  3. Perform duties assigned by the President.

Section 3: Secretary

  1. Convene meetings and issue notices.
  2. Record, keep, and distribute minutes.
  3. Maintain records and official correspondence.
  4. Communicate activities of the Association to members.

Section 4: Finance Officer / Treasurer

  1. Keep accurate financial records.
  2. Receive, safeguard, and deposit all funds.
  3. Present financial reports periodically.
  4. Ensure disbursement of funds is duly approved.
  5. Hand over records properly at the end of tenure.

Section 5: Organizer

  1. Mobilize members and coordinate activities.
  2. Organize meetings and social events.
  3. Arrange venues and logistics.
  4. Serve as Chief of Protocol.

Section 6: Patrons

  1. Patrons shall be members willing and able to support the Association financially and in any other capacity.
  2. The number of Patrons shall not be less than three (3).
  3. Any Executive Officer who completes a full term and is duly cleared may become a Patron, if willing.

ARTICLE VIII: AUTHORITY AND LIMITATIONS

Authority

The Executive Committee shall manage the affairs, assets, and finances of the Association and ensure the achievement of its objectives.

Limitations

  1. Spend above GH₵1,000 without approval of the General Assembly or Planning Committee.
  2. Dissolve the Association.
  3. Terminate membership without due process.

ARTICLE IX: ELECTIONS AND TENURE

  1. Elections shall be held every two (2) years, unless otherwise amended.
  2. Elections shall be supervised by an appointed Electoral Committee.
  3. Candidates must be members in good standing.
  4. Officers shall be elected by simple majority vote.
  5. No officer shall hold more than one executive position at the same time.
  6. Officers may serve a maximum of two (2) consecutive terms.

ARTICLE X: REMOVAL OF OFFICERS AND MEMBERS

  1. Officers may be removed for misconduct, misappropriation, violation of this Constitution, or persistent non-performance.
  2. Removal shall follow due process, written notice, and fair hearing.

ARTICLE XI: MEETINGS AND PROCEDURES

  1. General meetings shall be held at least twice each year.
  2. Emergency meetings may be convened by the President.
  3. Quorum for General Meetings shall be One-third (1/3) of members present.
  4. Meetings shall be conducted in English or any agreed local language.

ARTICLE XII: FINANCES, BENEFITS AND PROGRAMMES

Section 1: Contributions

  1. The Association shall operate a member contribution support system.
  2. Minimum contribution shall be GH₵100 (subject to review).
  3. Maximum contribution shall be voluntary.

Section 2: Beneficiaries

Members in good standing may benefit from support during:

  1. Marriage
  2. Death of a member
  3. Death of a parent
  4. Death of a spouse
  5. Death of a child
  6. Any other situation the Executive Committee may deem worthy of support.

Each approved event shall attract GH₵2,000, subject to availability of funds.

Section 3: Financial Controls

  1. The Association shall operate a bank account.
  2. Withdrawals shall require at least two authorized signatories.
  3. Accounts shall be audited internally at least twice yearly.

NOTICE FOR PROGRAMMES

A member shall inform the Association of a programme or situation that requires support ONLY AND OFFICIALLY THROUGH a member of the Executive Committee. The Executive member shall then table it before all members of the Executive Committee for a decision to be taken.

ARTICLE XIII: AMENDMENTS

  1. Any member may propose amendments in writing.
  2. Amendments shall be approved by the appropriate governing bodies.
  3. No amended provision shall be re-amended within six (6) months.

THE END
